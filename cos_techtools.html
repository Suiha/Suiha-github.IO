Curse of Strahd Zine



Technology & Tools



Clip Studio Paint


Clip Studio Paint (CSP) is a drawing program similar to Photoshop used for digital illustration and animation. I opted to use CSP instead of Photoshop due to my familiarity with the program, as well as access to free community tools such as custom brushes and patterns, as well as preset 3D models with adjustable light sources.



3D Modeling


CSP offers access to customizable 3D models, which I used to help sketch outlines for some of the pieces where I drew people. Since I struggle to draw people, this tool was invaluable for illustrating the pieces where I wanted to feature certain characters to represent specific concepts I had in mind. The CSP community also offers preset poses for models, which made it much simpler to create the specific pose I wanted.

Along with the adjustable 3D model, you can also adjust the light source, such as where it comes from and its intensity. This also greatly helped with determining shadows.

Using the 3D model, I could draw an outline of the figure I wanted and then color over that outline.



Custom Brushes & Patterns


You can also access custom brushes and patterns from CSP; these tools helped me save time during the drawing process, especially for certain textures and patterns I would have had to otherwise spend extra hours learning how to draw manually using a default brush.

For patterns, I could simply overlay them onto my base colors, adjust the angle and size of the patterns, and then recolor it. In CSP, patterns repeat infinitely no matter how much you resize or adjust them, making them much easier to work with than a transparent image of the same pattern.



Electric Zine Maker


Electric Zine Maker is a digital tool that provides templates to create zines. By using a zine maker, I did not have to worry about splicing my illustrations together to create the zine. I chose the smallest zine template with 8 pages, since I had about 2 weeks to complete this project.

If you click on any individual page, you can choose to import an image into the zine instead of using the zine maker as a drawing program.
