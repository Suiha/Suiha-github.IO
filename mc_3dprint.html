Minecraft & 3D Printing



Minecraft as a Medium




Minecraft is a 3D virtual world that is made up of various cubes (commonly known as "blocks"). These blocks can be collected and reused to build something non-native to the world.

Using a software called mineways, you can export your Minecraft build into a .stl file, which can then be loaded into a 3D printing software.





Exploding Boba in Minecraft


The original Minecraft build was made with 3D printing in mind, but without any experience with 3D printing.

I built a boba drink because it has been a constant in my life - I've been drinking it ever since it cost 3 dollars a cup, multiple times a month.

I made the build with the boba popping out and exploding from the drink; I thought it would look interesting in a 3D print, especially since the colors wouldn't carry over.

Draft Print of the Exploding Boba Build

Final Print of the Exploding Boba Build


When converting the build to a 3D print, many adjustments had to be made; I didn't realized that the "strands" of liquid and the boba "exploding" out of the top of the drink would create complications with the 3D build. The 3D printer would need to create physical supports to make sure those parts of the build had a foundation. In addition to the supports needed for the lid and the boba on the sides, the print would be almost fully covered by supports.

Instead of having boba flying out of the top, I moved the boba to rest on the lid. From there, the most difficult part was managing the supports on the sides of the cup. Due to all the protrusions on the sides, I had to revise the supports so that they wouldn't be built into the actual build itself, which would make it too difficult to remove them.
