NetArt



One form of new media art is net.art, or using websites to create art. Using HTML and GITHub, I made a simple website with multiple pages that link together in a linear fashion. Overall, the website is meant to highlight how people's curiosity will drive them to keep searching for new or hidden things, especially on the internet.

Click here to explore my netart page.



The landing page only has one word: "Hello." With no other visible interactable objects on the page, the viewer can only guess to click on "Hello," which is in such a big font that it is easy to see the mouse cursor indicate that there is a link. The next page is just as bland, though the viewer may suspect that one of the words is a hyperlink: similar to the first page, the link is unformatted, so they have to search with the mouse cursor to find it.

Especially curious viewers may try to search the rest of the page for something hidden, and discover several hidden links among the white background: someone who highlights the hidden cacophony of "here"s or uses dark mode will find a variety of references to DIO from Jojo's Bizarre Adventure.


The following page is similar to the previous one, with one key difference: a word is clearly highlighted as a hyperlink. However, it only links to a dead end of gifs - just like the previous page, the continuing link is "here."


From here, the website becomes much more linear, though the tone visibly shifts with the background becoming black and the text turning red. If you're not redirected to Google by saying that you'll go away, you'll be prompted to "pay" me using a credit card for more interactions. Eventually you'll be redirected to TikTok to look for cat videos.
